# Global-Sustainability-Metrics-Analysis


## Overview

This project explores the **ESG dataset**, which contains information about various countries and 17 key sustainability indicators across environmental, social, and governance categories. The goal is to understand how these factors interact over time and provide insights that can guide financial flows toward global sustainability objectives. This analysis focuses on the following indicators:

- Access to electricity (% of population)  
- Agriculture, forestry, and fishing, value added (% of GDP)  
- GDP growth (annual %)  
- Government expenditure on education, total (% of government expenditure)  
- Research and development expenditure (% of GDP)  
- Scientific and technical journal articles  
- Unemployment, total (% of total labor force) (modeled ILO estimate)  

The study examines trends and relationships for **Togo, Sub-Saharan Africa, and North America** over multiple decades.

---

## Data Collection

The dataset was sourced from the **World Bank Data Hub**, which provides comprehensive information on global development indicators. It includes yearly data from **1960 to 2023** for the indicators listed above, across multiple countries and regions.

---

## Data Wrangling and Exploration

Python libraries such as **Pandas** and **Seaborn** were used for data cleaning, exploration, and visualization. Various charts and plots were created to identify patterns, trends, and anomalies in the dataset.  

---

## Analysis

To investigate the relationships among the selected indicators, visualizations were created using **Matplotlib** and **Seaborn**. These visualizations allow users to compare trends across the three selected regions — **Togo, Sub-Saharan Africa, and North America** — and explore how environmental, social, and governance factors relate to each other over time.

---

## Libraries Used

- **Pandas** – for data handling and manipulation  
- **NumPy** – for numerical calculations  
- **Seaborn** – for statistical data visualization  
- **Matplotlib.pyplot** – for plotting and visualization  

---

## Conclusion

This analysis provides insights into the dynamics of ESG-related indicators across the selected countries and regions. Key takeaways include:

- The level of access to electricity and its growth trends over time  
- The contribution of agriculture, forestry, and fishing to GDP  
- GDP growth patterns and their correlation with education and R&D expenditures  
- Research output and its potential link to social and economic development  
- Unemployment trends and labor force participation  

The visualizations offer a clear, comparative understanding of these indicators for **Togo, Sub-Saharan Africa, and North America**, highlighting regional differences and potential areas for policy focus.
